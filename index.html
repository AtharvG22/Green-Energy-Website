Design a rooftop solar PV system for a residential house (5 kW)

Considering storage and grid connection

Block Diagram

Explanation

Source of energy: The primary energy source is sunlight harvested by rooftop photovoltaic (PV) modules rated at 5 kW peak. The grid serves as a secondary source/backup and sink for exported energy.

Conversion process: Solar panels produce DC electricity. Strings feed a DC combiner with surge and DC isolation protection. A hybrid inverter with PPT converts PV DC to AC for household loads, manages battery charging/discharging, and synchronizes with the grid for export/import. The battery bank stores DC energy; a BMS and battery protection circuitry manage safe charging and depth-of-discharge.

Output/utilization: The inverter supplies an AC distribution panel that feeds household appliances. During sunny hours PV powers loads and charges batteries; excess power can be sent to the grid via the net meter. At night or under low solar, batteries supply loads, and the grid provides backup if batteries are depleted.

Real-world relevance: This architecture is common for residential decarbonization‚Äîreducing electricity bills, enhancing reliability during outages (if inverter supports islanding), and enabling rooftop owners to participate in net-metering policies. It balances self-consumption with grid interaction and is scalable to larger systems.

Develop the block diagram of a solar water heating system for a university hostel with hot water .

demand of 2000 liters/day

Block Diagram

Explanation

Source of energy: Solar thermal energy collected from rooftop solar collectors (evacuated tube or flat-plate) is the primary energy source. A backup (electric or gas) supplies heat during prolonged low-sun periods.

Conversion process: Sunlight heats the collector surface which warms a heat transfer fluid in the collector loop. For reliability, a closed glycol loop often circulates between the collectors and a heat exchanger inside a well-insulated storage tank. A differential temperature controller activates the circulation pump when collector temperature exceeds tank temperature by a set delta, maximizing heat pickup while preventing stagnation.

Output/utilization: Heated potable water stored in insulated tanks supplies showers, kitchens, and laundry across the hostel. The heat exchanger keeps the potable loop separate if antifreeze is used. Backup heating maintains temperature during insufficient solar gain.

Real-world relevance: Centralized solar water heating reduces energy bills and peak electrical loads for institutions with high hot-water demand. It is practical for hostels, dormitories, and hotels where predictable daily loads (2,000 L/day) enable efficient collector and tank sizing.

Solar-Powered Irrigation Pumping System

Off-grid Agriculture

Block Diagram

Explanation

Source of energy: The direct energy source is solar photovoltaic electricity from ground-mounted or rooftop PV arrays sized to meet daily irrigation energy requirements. Optionally, a battery bank stores energy to support pumping during low-insolation periods or to schedule pumping at non-sunlight hours.

Conversion process: The PV array output is regulated using an MPPT controller to maximize energy harvest. For DC solar pumps, a DC-DC PPT-driven pump controller directly drives the motor. For conventional AC pumps, a solar inverter converts PV DC to grid-compatible AC. A soft-start or motor controller manages inrush current and matches pump operating point to available power. If batteries are used, the BMS and charge controllers manage charging and provide a steady supply to the motor.

Output/utilization: The pump fills an elevated water storage tank or directly supplies pressurized irrigation lines with filters and control valves. Stored water enables gravity-fed irrigation schedules, improving distribution uniformity and allowing irrigation when sunlight isn‚Äôt available.

Real-world relevance: Off-grid solar pumping reduces diesel usage and operating costs for small farms, improves reliability, and enables sustainable irrigation in water-scarce rural areas. Systems scale from small household plots to community irrigation schemes.
